# Compliance Grid — Translation & Accuracy Notes

Generated during content build. Source: Central Bank of Cyprus AML/CFT Directive of 2025 (K.Δ.Π. 120/2025), read from the original Greek PDF and translated into English for this training app.

**Recommended before wider rollout:** have the company's AMLCO do one accuracy pass on the question bank below, per the build spec. This is real compliance training material, not just a game.

## Verification process

Each chapter was drafted from the translated source articles, then independently fact-checked by two reviewers (citation accuracy; threshold/percentage and mandatory-vs-discretionary framing), then any flagged issues were corrected against source. Corrections made during that pass are listed per chapter below, followed by translation ambiguities noted during drafting.

## Foundations

**Corrections made during verification (3):**

- Glossary term 'Compliance Officer': removed the unsupported added clause '...which also distinguish the role from a Deputy Compliance Officer' — the ground-truth exact_facts for Article 2 define the Compliance Officer solely as 'the natural person appointed under Article 69(a) of the Law' and do not state that Article 2 draws any Compliance Officer/Deputy Compliance Officer distinction; the definition was tightened to state only what the source supports.
- Glossary term 'Management body': restored the omitted clause 'and which includes the person who actually directs the entity's business activities,' which is part of Article 2's actual definition (and already correctly reflected in Q19) but had been dropped from the glossary entry, making it an incomplete statement of who counts as the management body.
- All 20 questions (and their followups), the briefing, and the remaining glossary entries were checked against the ground-truth source articles (citations, euro/percentage thresholds, must/shall vs. may framing, and marked-correct options) and found accurate; no other changes were made.

**Translation ambiguities / judgment calls flagged (5):**

- Article 1 (short title) does not fit the mandatory/discretionary schema used elsewhere in the Directive; it is classified as 'definitional' for game-mechanic purposes, and no shall/must or may language is asserted for it in this chapter's content.
- Article 2 is an extensive interpretation clause (source pages roughly 795-799) containing 20+ defined terms. Only definitions carrying independent legal or numeric significance (e.g., the 25% substantial-interest threshold, dated EU instruments, cross-references) were built into full questions here; several minor cross-referential definitions (e.g., currency exchange bureaus, agents, distributors, financial-leasing businesses as defined terms) were not turned into standalone questions. A compliance officer should confirm none of the omitted definitions carry outcome-determinative significance before this chapter is treated as a complete substitute for reading Article 2 in full.
- Article 3's list of obliged-entity categories runs (a) to (f)/(στ) and then jumps to (h)/(η); no (g)/(ζ) subparagraph could be located in the reviewed source pages (798-799). This may reflect a repeal or renumbering by one of the many amending instruments cited in the preamble, but its content (if any) could not be verified from the material provided. This gap is surfaced directly to the player in Q6 as a 'flag for sign-off' exercise rather than presented as settled fact, and a compliance officer should confirm before this chapter goes live that no substantive obligation is being silently omitted.
- Article 4 is a declaratory purpose clause; it does not itself use 'shall/must' or 'may' language, so it has been treated as background/context (obligation_type: 'other') rather than as a testable mandatory rule, consistent with the source material's own classification. Q18 tests this classification explicitly as a 'why is this not mandatory/discretionary' synthesis question.
- No translation ambiguities were flagged in the source material provided for Article 5.

- Verified the translation_ambiguity_notes' claim about Article 17 ('Annual Report of the Compliance Officer', Part 5) against the full directive index entry for Art. 17 — confirmed the 3-month/31-March deadline and 1-month board-minutes-to-CBC deadline described there genuinely mirror Article 23's Risk Recording and Assessment Report deadlines, so the note is accurate and was kept unchanged.
- Strengthened (did not change the correct answer for) rr-10's correct_feedback/incorrect_feedback: the original text grounded the 'Enhanced' answer solely in the Article 20 clause barring Simplified due diligence, which on its own only establishes that Simplified is barred, not that Enhanced (rather than Standard) specifically applies. Added the explicit link to Article 20's incorporation of 'paragraphs 42-51 of the Directive' (the Enhanced due diligence / unusual-transaction-pattern provisions), which is what actually supports Enhanced as the required tier for an indicated structuring attempt.
- No changes needed to briefing, glossary_terms_used, or the remaining translation_ambiguity_notes — all verified accurate against source.

**Translation ambiguities / judgment calls flagged (5):**

- Source translation note on Art. 19: 'Ολιστική άποψη' is rendered 'holistic view'; 'σταθμίζω τους παράγοντες κινδύνου' as 'weigh the risk factors', reflecting the risk-scoring/weighting methodology described. Sign-off should confirm 'weigh' is read as an active scoring/methodology step (not merely 'consider'), given the operative constraints that follow it (no undue single-factor influence, no profit-motive distortion, no structural exclusion of high-risk ratings).
- Article 64(1)(α)(β)(γ) (referenced from Art. 20) uses Greek-letter subparagraph lettering (α=alpha, β=beta, γ=gamma) carried over from the source Law. Recommend confirming that in-game citations preserve this Greek lettering rather than silently substituting Latin (a)(b)(c), since a compliance officer cross-checking against the official Greek-language Law text will look for the Greek letters specifically.
- Article 23's 'Risk Recording and Assessment Report' (Part 6) and Article 17's 'Annual Report of the Compliance Officer' (Part 5, referenced in the compact index but outside this chapter's primary source range) are two distinct documents that happen to share an almost identical submission architecture — a 3-month/31-March deadline to the board and CBC, and board minutes to the CBC within 1 month. Because both are prepared by the Compliance Officer on the same annual cycle, recommend sign-off confirm that in-game text does not let players conflate the two reports or believe one filing satisfies both obligations.
- The source materials use 'obliged entities' in the Part 6 text extracted for this chapter, but 'liable entities' appears in later parts of the compact index (e.g., Parts 7 onward) for what appears to be the same defined class of CBC-supervised persons under Article 3. This may reflect two different translation passes over the Greek original rather than a substantive distinction between terms; recommend standardizing on one term (e.g., 'obliged entities') across the whole game rather than mirroring the source's inconsistency.
- The phrase 'as soon as issued or known' (Art. 22, governing review of terrorism-alert and sanctions updates) is a direct rendering of the source's urgency standard and does not specify a numeric SLA (e.g., same business day, 24 hours). Recommend compliance sign-off confirm whether player-facing feedback should preserve this open-ended 'as soon as' standard as-is, rather than implying a specific numeric deadline that isn't in the source text.

## The Gate

**Corrections made during verification (1):**

- No issues found. Every question and followup_question in this chapter was checked against the ground-truth source articles (24-41, cross-referencing Article 42 and the full directive index): article/part citations, euro thresholds (the EUR 1,000 currency-exchange trigger in gate-q1), time periods (the 2-year management-accounts rule in gate-q2, the 6-month utility-bill age limit in gate-q6/q7, the 12-month PEP look-back reflected in the glossary), must/shall vs. may framing (e.g. the discretionary core of Articles 38/39 vs. their mandatory safeguards in gate-q17; the conjunctive mandatory-refusal test of Article 37 in gate-q11/q13; the discretionary-but-conditional nature of Simplified Due Diligence in gate-q9/q10), and every marked-correct option/node were verified word-for-word against the source and found accurate. No factual corrections were necessary.

**Translation ambiguities / judgment calls flagged (6):**

- Article 32(4) cross-references 'paragraph 29(7)' of the Directive for address-verification methods for this customer cohort, but Article 29 (reliance on third parties) contains no subparagraph (7) on address verification — that content actually appears in Article 30(6). This cross-reference inconsistency is preserved exactly as it appears in the source Gazette text rather than silently corrected, and should be confirmed with a compliance officer before publishing any in-game text that names a specific article number for this address-verification route.
- Article 37 contains two separately-numbered '(14)' subparagraphs in sequence in the original Gazette text (one on further verification triggered by later ownership/structure changes, another on parent-company ownership verification). This appears to be a numbering artifact in the source rather than a duplication of substance, and has been treated as two distinct provisions; worth a compliance officer's confirmation against the official Gazette PDF in case it reflects a corrigendum.
- Article 41's permission to apply Simplified Due Diligence is discretionary ('δύνανται'), but nearly all of the surrounding conditions and the express prohibition on applying SDD in certain circumstances are mandatory. In-game copy describing SDD as something an entity 'can' use should make clear this is a conditional, evidence-gated allowance, not a standing entitlement to skip due diligence — otherwise trainees may infer a blanket carve-out that does not exist.
- Question gate-q18 relies on Article 32's statement that 'standard CDD and ongoing monitoring obligations still apply to all such customers, proportionate to risk' to establish that the special identification-document regime for asylum applicants/refugees/trafficking victims does not, by itself, change the CDD tier. This is an explicit textual statement rather than an inference, but is flagged for sign-off because it runs counter to an intuitive (and incorrect) assumption that alternative-document customers automatically receive lighter-touch treatment.
- The nodegrid item (gate-q11) classifying an 'ordinary retail customer with no risk flags and no formal low-risk determination' as 'medium' risk is a pedagogical convention for this training game, not a label used explicitly anywhere in Articles 24-41 of the source text. The Directive's actual structure in this chapter only explicitly names a confirmed-low-risk category (Article 41) and a classified-high-risk category (Article 27, pointing to Article 42); everything else defaults to full/standard due diligence. Flagging this game-design inference for compliance sign-off so it is not mistaken for a literal source classification.
- Two overlapping extractions of Article 28 appear in the source materials for this chapter (one a partial version truncated mid-paragraph (1), one a fuller version covering paragraphs (1)-(6)). No question in this chapter's bank is drawn from Article 28's substantive content (it falls slightly outside the core CDD-tier focus), but a compliance officer should confirm which extraction is authoritative before Article 28 content is used in a later chapter, to avoid double-counting or gaps.

## Enhanced Measures

**Corrections made during verification (3):**

- eq_06: Changed the correct tier from 'Standard' to 'Enhanced'. Article 42(1)(b) lists 'client accounts held in a third party's name' as one of four mandatory enhanced-due-diligence triggers, and Article 42 separately states that all cases in paragraphs 43-51 of the Directive (which include Article 45's client-account provisions) 'must always be treated/classified as high risk' with the EDD measures specified in the relevant article applied. A real estate agent's Article 45 client account does not meet the Directive's narrow simplified-due-diligence carve-out (reserved for EEA/low-risk-country credit institutions and National Betting Authority licensees meeting strict cumulative conditions), so it defaults to Enhanced due diligence, not the generic 'Standard' due diligence the draft incorrectly assigned -- this also brings the question in line with the draft's own eq_05 nodegrid, which correctly tiers any non-carved-out third-party-name client account as 'high'. Rewrote the correct/incorrect feedback and added Art. 42 to the article citation.
- eq_07: Corrected the scenario's illustrative example, which cited 'certain government departments operating pooled accounts' as an example of the professional-intermediary (non-'third party' under Article 67(2)) category carrying the EUR 10,000 threshold. Per the source, government departments' pooled/general accounts actually carry the Directive's separate, higher EUR 15,000 threshold -- a distinct category from the EUR 10,000 professional-intermediary threshold. Rewrote the scenario so it no longer conflates the two categories/thresholds, and added a clarifying sentence to the correct/incorrect feedback distinguishing the government-department pooled-account threshold (EUR 15,000) from the non-'third party' professional-intermediary threshold (EUR 10,000).
- All other 18 questions (including followup_questions), all glossary entries, and all translation_ambiguity_notes were checked against the ground-truth source articles (Art. 42-51, plus cross-referenced articles from the full directive index) and found accurate: euro thresholds, review frequencies (monthly PEP scan, annual high-risk review), mandatory vs. discretionary framing, article/part citations, and named sources (FATF, FSAP, EBA Guidelines, MOKAS) all matched the source text. No further changes were made.

**Translation ambiguities / judgment calls flagged (6):**

- Article 45's provisions on 'client accounts' were extracted across two separate page ranges in the source material, and the article's opening heading/paragraphs preceding the second excerpt were not captured. The title used for the second portion is inferred from context rather than transcribed from a visible heading. A compliance officer should verify the full, continuous text of Article 45 against the official Gazette before this chapter's Article 45 content is treated as a standalone authoritative reference.
- The source material states a EUR 15,000 beneficial-owner verification threshold for both (a) client accounts of the enumerated professions (auditor/accountant/tax adviser, lawyer, TCSP, real estate agent) and (b) general pooled 'client accounts' with sub-account capability, but a separately-extracted continuation of Article 45 also lists a EUR 10,000 threshold specifically described as applying to 'pooled accounts/sub-accounts'. It is not fully clear from the extracted material whether this reflects two genuinely distinct account categories with different thresholds, or a transcription/translation inconsistency across the two excerpts. Because euro thresholds are legally material facts that must never be approximated, this specific discrepancy (EUR 15,000 vs EUR 10,000 for pooled/sub-account beneficial-owner verification) should be resolved against the official Greek Gazette text and signed off by a compliance officer before this chapter is finalized for production use. In this chapter's questions, the EUR 10,000 figure has been used only where the source unambiguously ties it to a distinct category (professional intermediaries who are NOT 'third parties' under Article 67(2) of the Law), to avoid relying on the ambiguous figure.
- Article 42's obligation_type is marked 'mixed' in the source analysis: the overarching duty to apply enhanced due diligence is mandatory, but the specific list of EDD techniques in its final subparagraph is introduced as an indicative, non-exhaustive ('may include') list. This chapter's questions treat the four relationship categories in Article 42(1) as mandatory triggers, and the specific technique menu as illustrative rather than an exhaustive checklist -- this framing should be confirmed by the compliance officer responsible for translating it into operational policy.
- Article 43's option to monitor individual transactions is explicitly discretionary ('may decide'), while the surrounding duty to identify and respond to unusual transactions is mandatory. This chapter tests that specific mandatory/discretionary split as a distinct fact; a sign-off should confirm this nuance is not lost when simplified for learners.
- Article 50's treatment of customers/beneficial owners with 'close personal or professional ties' to a high-risk third country (short of the five explicit 'connected' triggers) uses discretionary framing for whether to apply enhanced or normal due diligence. This chapter's nodegrid question represents that category as 'medium' risk for pedagogical purposes, to distinguish it from the automatically-high explicit triggers -- but the Directive itself does not use a three-tier low/medium/high labelling scheme for this specific provision. A compliance officer should confirm this simplification is acceptable for training purposes.
- The English rendering of Article 44's title retains 'foundations' in parentheses, mirroring how the source itself parenthetically glosses the Greek term -- flagged only as a terminology note, not a substantive legal ambiguity.

## The Flag

**Corrections made during verification (1):**

- No issues found

**Translation ambiguities / judgment calls flagged (6):**

- Article 54's prohibition on accepting undeclared imported cash of €10,000+ is translated from a strong Greek prohibition ('Απαγορεύεται') and rendered as an absolute prohibition rather than a softer restriction — compliance sign-off should confirm no discretion is intended for edge cases.
- Article 55's cumulative calendar-year threshold ('exceed, or are expected to exceed, €100,000') leaves the exact trigger point for a forward-looking 'expected to exceed' judgment undefined in the source text — at what point in a pattern of smaller deposits the entity must treat the cumulative total as 'expected to exceed' the threshold is a judgment call for the Compliance Officer, not a bright-line rule as drafted.
- Article 56 is drafted as an exemption/carve-out ('the requirements... do not apply') rather than using shall/may obligation language, so it is classified as obligation_type 'other' rather than mandatory or discretionary — compliance sign-off should confirm this exemption is read narrowly, strictly limited to the three listed categories with no implied extensions.
- Article 66's prohibition on 'tipping off' the client is a standard AML/CFT-terminology rendering rather than a literal translation of the Greek text, included for clarity since it is the universally understood term for this obligation; the underlying legal source is Article 48 of the Law, cross-referenced but not itself reproduced in this chapter's source extract.
- Article 66 requires Suspicion Reports to be submitted 'within a reasonable timeframe' per MOKAS's own instructions 'from time to time' — no fixed number of days is specified in the source text, so operational SLAs for 'reasonable' must be set by reference to MOKAS's current guidance rather than treated as fixed by this Directive itself.
- No 'tiergate' (Simplified/Standard/Enhanced) question was built for this chapter: this chapter's assigned source articles (Part 8 cash thresholds, Part 12 suspicious-activity reporting) do not themselves specify a CDD tier decision — Article 55 only requires that 'full CDD (per Part 7)' be complete before approving a large cash transaction, without stating which tier applies — so no tier classification was asserted beyond what the source text supports.

## Mechanics Overhaul — interaction-layer sprint (2026-07-12)

Per the sprint's own build prompt (`Compliance-Grid-Mechanics-Master-Spec.md`), this pass replaced the interaction layer (which mechanic the player uses to answer) without inventing, changing, or deleting any content fact, citation, euro threshold, or correct answer. Every question below kept its original `id`, citation(s), and correct answer; only `mechanic` (and, for a few items, the on-screen framing) changed. Two content-level fixes were also made, both cosmetic/organizational, not factual:

- **Glossary deduplication**: PEP, EDD, UBO (each had 2-3 near-identical entries, per the existing note above) plus CDD, SDD, CBC, EBA, EEA, MiCA (same issue, not previously flagged) were consolidated to one entry each, keeping the fuller/more precise definition and merging source citations. No definition content was altered beyond merging.
- **`the_flag` difficulty order**: was out of easy→medium→hard sequence (a medium item sat among the easy ones, and a medium item followed three hard ones). Reordered; no citations or correct answers changed.

**Content restructuring (repackaging existing verified facts into new interaction shapes, nothing invented):**

- `foundations-q2` (Senior management) and `foundations-q12` ("the Law") were merged into a single 5-pair **Match the term** question, adding UBO, Source of Wealth, and Source of Funds as three more pairs — all five definitions are the same verbatim, already-verified glossary text. `foundations-q12` no longer exists as a standalone slot.
- `rr-01` (Article 18's two-assessment requirement) became a **Match the term** question pairing "Business-wide risk assessment" / "Individual risk assessment" — the same glossary definitions already used elsewhere in this chapter.
- `eq_01` (annual high-risk review frequency) became a 3-pair **Match the term** question (PEP / EDD / SDD) — trades the frequency fact for direct definitional coverage of three terms this content rule specifically calls out as under-tested.
- `eq_08` (shell bank vs. correspondent respondent) became a **Match the term** pairing "Shell bank" / "Shell company" — the same Article 2-of-the-Law / Article 37 distinction, same citations.
